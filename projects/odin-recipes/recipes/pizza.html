Pizza

Description

Pizza (Italian: [ˈpittsa], Neapolitan: [ˈpittsə]) is an Italian dish consisting of a usually round, flattened base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and often various other ingredients (such as anchovies, mushrooms, onions, olives, pineapple, meat, etc.), which is then baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven.

In Italy, pizza served in formal settings, such as at a restaurant, is presented unsliced, and is eaten with the use of a knife and fork.[2][3] In casual settings, however, it is cut into wedges to be eaten while held in the hand.

Ingredients

  • Pre-made pizza dough
  • Tomato sauce
  • Mozarella cheese
  • Basil leaves
  • Mushrooms
  • Pepperoni

Steps

  • Roll pizza dough into circular shape
  • Spread tomato sauce over dough
  • Spread shredded cheese over tomato sauce
  • Evenly distribute other ingredients
  • Bake in 400F deg oven for 20 minutes
